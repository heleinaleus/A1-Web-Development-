Chocolate Crinkle Cookies

Baked with love, crinkled to perfection.

By: Maria Heleina Leus

These soft-baked delights that resembles brownies in cookie form are amazing during the festive season. It's a classic holiday treat that is almost as fun to make as it is to eat!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup natural unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 cup powdered sugar

Method

  1. Beat together the cocoa powder, white sugar, and vegetable oil:

    2. In a bowl of an electric mixer, beat cocoa powder, white sugar, and vegetable oil with the paddle attachment or by hand with a wooden spoon until it forms a shiny, gritty, black dough.

  2. Add the eggs and then the vanilla:

    3. One at a time, mixing for thirty seconds each, add the eggs and vanilla and in mix thoroughly.

  3. Whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt:

    4. In a seperate bowl, together the flour, baking powder and salt.

  4. Beat the dry ingredients into the cocoa-oil mix:

    5. Mix in the chocolate mixture on low speed until just combined. Do not over-beat.

  5. Cover and chill:

    6. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and chill it for four hours or overnight.

  6. Preheat the oven and prepare the baking sheets:

    7. Preheat the oven to 350°F. Line two baking pans with parchment paper. Place the powdered sugar in a bowl.

  7. Roll the cookies:

    8. Scoop out clumps of the chilled dough with a rounded teaspoon, then use your hands to roll them into 1-inch (2.5 cm) balls. Drop them into the powdered sugar as you go, doing a few at a time. Place the balls on the cookie sheets, a few inches apart, after rolling them in the powdered sugar.

  8. Bake at 350°F for 10 to 13 minutes:

    9. Bake for 10 to 13 minutes at 350°F. Let it cool on the sheets for a minute or two, then move to a wire rack to cool completely. Repeat with any leftover batter.
